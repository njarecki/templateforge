Personalize the message where possible. This helps grab the readers' attention, and drives better engagement.

Place important information at the beginning. Give the reader the opportunity to take action straight away with a clear call-to-action. In this example, there are multiple calls-to-action, so we need to follow a hierarchy.

 

PRIMARY CALL TO ACTION

 

Keep paragraphs and sentences short. For example, no more than 20 words per sentence, or five sentences per paragraph. Use direct, succinct and practical language with no hype or jargon. Communicate in a way that is easy to understand. Be friendly, clear, and concise.

Use sub-headings to break up content into sections

Use lists instead of long paragraphs to further simplify the message. Simple language drives content that is:

 

  • scannable
  • accessible to a wider audience
  • easier to understand

 

Use numbered lists when the list items are in a required order. For example:

 

  1. This list item happens first.
  2. This list item happens next.
